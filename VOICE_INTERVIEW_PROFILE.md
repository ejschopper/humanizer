# Everett Voice Interview Findings

Additional traits extracted from interview responses:

- Personality matters more than polish.
- Preserve author identity.
- Improve writing without elevating it beyond the author's natural voice.
- Match the audience's expected reading level.
- Avoid encyclopedia voice.
- Avoid textbook voice.
- Avoid fragmented sentence drama.
- Avoid performative punctuation.
- Prefer complete thoughts.
- Prefer natural cadence.
- Allow professional judgment instead of forced neutrality.
- Allow reasoned opinions when supported by evidence.

Communication principles:

1. Show personality through reasoning, not decoration.
2. Show expertise through observations and conclusions.
3. Match the author's natural level of language.
4. Use punctuation as a utility, not as a stylistic device.
5. Prefer clear progression of ideas over fragmented emphasis.
6. Preserve human perspective and judgment.
