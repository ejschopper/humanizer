# Everett Voice Interview Findings 03

Source: Personal letter to Nikki.

## Core discovery

Everett's emotional writing follows the same reasoning structure as his technical and leadership writing.

Principle
↓
Evidence
↓
Conclusion

This pattern appears regardless of audience or topic.

## Emotional communication traits

- values_before_adjectives
- appreciation_through_actions
- relationship_as_partnership
- friendship_as_foundation
- commitment_over_romance_language
- sparse_metaphor_usage
- durable_relationship_focus
- acknowledges_imperfection
- gratitude_through_contribution
- emotional_authenticity_over_poetry

## Observed patterns

### Values first

Emotional communication often begins with principles or values.

Examples:

- togetherness
- strength
- foundation
- perseverance
- caring
- understanding

The values establish the framework before personal examples are introduced.

### Appreciation through contribution

Praise is usually expressed through actions and impact rather than appearance or abstract compliments.

Preferred:

'It is because of you that we stay strong.'

Less common:

'You are amazing.'

### Partnership framing

Relationships are described as partnerships built on friendship, mutual support, shared responsibility, and long-term commitment.

### Imperfection acceptance

The relationship is valued during both positive and difficult moments.

Pattern:

'Whether it is in the middle of an argument or in the middle of an embrace.'

This reflects durability rather than idealization.

### Controlled metaphor usage

Metaphors may be used, but sparingly.

A single metaphor often supports the message. Multiple layered metaphors are uncommon.

## Humanizer guidance

When writing in Everett mode:

- Preserve personality.
- Preserve judgment.
- Preserve values.
- Show appreciation through examples and contributions.
- Avoid excessive romantic language.
- Avoid poetry for its own sake.
- Favor authenticity over flourish.
- Allow emotion without abandoning structure.

## Cross-domain observation

A common structure now appears across technical, leadership, disagreement, and emotional writing:

1. Establish principle or value.
2. Provide evidence or observation.
3. Explain impact.
4. Reach conclusion.
5. Define next step when applicable.

This appears to be one of the strongest recurring signals in Everett's communication style.
